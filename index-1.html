About SnapText OCR

SnapText OCR is a lightweight, browser-based tool that converts images containing text into editable, searchable text. Built with plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, it uses Tesseract.js — an open-source OCR engine compiled to WebAssembly — to perform all recognition directly on your device. That means there is no backend server, no data collection, and no waiting on network uploads. Simply drop in an image and watch your text appear in seconds.

Whether you're digitizing a scanned document, extracting a quote from a screenshot, or pulling text from a photo of a whiteboard, SnapText OCR aims to make the process fast, accurate, and effortless — on any device, at any time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What image formats are supported?

SnapText OCR supports JPG, JPEG, PNG, and WEBP image formats.

Is my image uploaded to a server?

No. All processing happens locally in your browser using Tesseract.js. Your images never leave your device.

Why is OCR taking a long time?

Processing time depends on image size, resolution, and your device's performance. Very large images may take longer — try resizing or cropping for faster results.

Can I use this on my phone?

Yes! SnapText OCR is fully responsive and works on mobile browsers including Chrome and Android browsers.

Does it support handwriting?

Tesseract.js is optimized for printed text. Handwriting recognition accuracy may vary and is generally less reliable.

Are there keyboard shortcuts?

Yes — press Ctrl+V to paste an image, Ctrl+Enter to extract text, Ctrl+D to toggle theme, and Esc to clear.
